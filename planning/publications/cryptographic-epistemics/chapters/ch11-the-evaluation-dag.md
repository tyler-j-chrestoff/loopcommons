# Chapter 11: The Evaluation DAG

Every soul mutation must carry its proof of fitness. The Merkle chain isn't linear — it's a DAG, because three populations co-evolve (souls, encounters, evaluators). Two-tier evaluation: stratified sieve for fast generations (~30% sample), full battery at checkpoints. Cross-evaluator sampling breaks collusion. Three-way failure decomposition: was it the soul, the evaluator, or the encounter? Performance-gated decay for the failure pool — exit on demonstrated competence, not time. The forensic principle: diagnosis, not rollback.
