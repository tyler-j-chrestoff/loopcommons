# Chapter 7: Two Boundaries

There are exactly two points where continuous becomes discrete in this architecture: (1) the decoder boundary — continuous latent navigation collapses into a discrete artifact (instantiation), and (2) the evaluator boundary — continuous behavioral execution collapses into a discrete judgment (scoring). Both are epistemic events. Both must be hashed. Develop the claim: continuous spaces are for imagination, discrete commitments are for knowledge. The bridge between them is where intelligence becomes accountable.
