# Loop Commons

## Vision

**AI that knows who it is, serving people the system forgot.**

The frontier labs are building general intelligence for everyone. We're building specific intelligence for someone — the veteran navigating the VA at 2am, the kid aging out of foster care with no one to call, the person sleeping outside who needs to know where the warming shelter opened tonight.

These communities don't need a more powerful model. They need an agent that shows up on the channels they already use, remembers what they told it last week, can't be tricked into giving harmful advice, and earns trust through transparency — not terms of service.

## Mission

Loop Commons builds open-source AI agents grounded in identity, accountable by design, and deployed in service of Denver's underserved communities.

We do this through three commitments:

**1. Agents that know themselves.**
An agent without identity is a tool anyone can aim. Our agents carry their values in their architecture — not as rules that can be overridden, but as structural properties of the system. Security through self-knowledge. A compressed identity that survives every interaction because it's load-bearing, not decorative.

**2. Auditable by default.**
Every decision an agent makes has an energy cost, recorded on a ledger that balances. Not because we're required to. Because the communities we serve have been lied to by every system they've ever touched. When a formerly unhoused person asks "why did you tell me that?", the answer isn't "the model hallucinated." The answer is a receipt.

**3. Community is the unit.**
We don't optimize individual agents. We cultivate ecosystems of specialists that cover the problem space together — housing, healthcare, legal aid, crisis response. Fitness isn't strength. Fitness is fit. The measure of our system is whether the community it serves is better covered today than yesterday.

## Principles

**Build the mirror, not the wall.** Container isolation keeps the world out. Substrate awareness lets the agent see itself. Both are necessary. Only one creates trust.

**Dead agents are data.** When an approach fails, that failure maps the landscape. Export it, learn from it, share it. The non-profit's graveyard is more valuable than its trophy case.

**Energy is finite and shared.** The same ledger tracks an agent's internal subsystem economy and the non-profit's API budget. Thermodynamics doesn't lie. When you can see where the energy goes, you can see where the care goes.

**The architecture is the argument.** We don't write whitepapers about how AI should serve humanity. We build agents that do it, trace every decision, and publish the results. The code is the claim. The ledger is the proof.
